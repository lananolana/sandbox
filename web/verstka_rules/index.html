Four Markup Rules

Emptiness

A simple rule that is often broken: put elements with similar meaning next to each other and surround them with empty space. Emptiness is a powerful means of influence.

Grid

The sizes of the elements and the distances between them fit into a harmonious pattern - this is called a grid. If an element falls out, the viewer can notice it.

Fonts

No more than three fonts per site. One for headlines, one for all other texts, and one for emphasizing important words. That should be enough.

Colors

There are usually two colors on a site: a leading color and an accent color. To choose a successful color pairing can be due to the eyes or special tools.
